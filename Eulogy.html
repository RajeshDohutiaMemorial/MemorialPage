Eulogy

"Dada...a stable, good-looking, accomplished young boy. My early memories of him are those of being protected by him, of being carried home in his lap when I'd fall asleep in the car on the way back from late-night trips to Jatinga, of being teased no end. I'd get dressed for school, hair neatly tied in two ponytails, shoes polished, only to have my scrunchie pulled and hair messed up precisely when it was time to leave for school. He would play big brother to my friends and tease them equally. He'd be there, looking out for me, my sister, and ma and protecting us. I would be amiss if I didn't acknowledge that the Dada I knew in the last 15 years was a troubled soul. I am trying to comfort myself by telling myself that he is at peace now, in a beautiful place...farewell, my brother, may your spirit be free. I hope you know that you are loved."

- Shabnam Dohutia (Bipi)

"O'Raju, as I always use to call him over phone or physically was a man loaded with good IQ and vibrant words. He was a kind person by heart and always use to think for the downtrodden people in our society. He was a commerce graduate with a management degree to his name. During his early days he worked for companies like Dabur, Eveready, ITA (Assam Branch), NETA etc at different positions. I knew him because he was my maternal Jethpaha's younger sister's (Bindu Pahi) son. Moreover, his In-laws residence is at a walkable distance from our home. Though I am quite busy in my schedule as a self employed person I use to take time out to meet him and converse with him regarding the positive aspects of our daily life. I always use to encourage him to get himself busy by doing some responsible work consistently. But I believe destiny had some other plan and as such he was diverted in another route which we humans have to surrender. At last but not the least O'Raju will be in my heart as a good friend of mine. May Almighty keep him in a corridor close to him and be like a twinkling star in the sky. I pray to God for the eternal peace of the departed soul and a peaceful future for the entire family."

- Mriganka Barua
